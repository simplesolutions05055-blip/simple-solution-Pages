# Ad Design — Paid Creative Direction

Visual production for paid social and display ads. Output is a complete creative brief + final design files (or AI-generation prompts + Figma-ready overlays) that a media buyer can ship to Meta Ads Manager / TikTok Ads / Google Ads the same day.

## Philosophy

**The visual is the second the scroll stops.** In the first 0.3 seconds the brain decides "interesting / boring" — and that decision is purely visual. Clever copy never gets read on a boring image. Visual hook beats clever copy every time.

A second principle that controls 80% of performance on social: **native-feel beats polished agency look.** On TikTok and Reels, an ad that looks like a friend's iPhone post outperforms a glossy studio shoot by 3-5x. On Meta feed it's closer, but native still wins. The "expensive-looking ad" is dead in 2026. Save polish for brand campaigns; for direct response, design ads that camouflage as content.

## Brief intake (12 questions)

Ask before touching pixels:

1. **Product / service** — what exactly is being sold, in one sentence
2. **Ad objective** — awareness / consideration / conversion / app install / lead-form
3. **Platform mix** — Meta feed, Stories, Reels, TikTok, YouTube, Google Display, WhatsApp Status (rank by budget share)
4. **Format mix** — static, carousel, video, motion graphics, UGC-style (specify count per format)
5. **Brand assets available** — logo (SVG preferred), brand colors (hex), brand fonts, product photography, lifestyle photography, brand guidelines doc
6. **Audience demographics** — age, gender, income, interests + **Israeli regional cues**: תל אביב hipster vs באר שבע משפחתי vs ירושלים שמרני vs צפון/קיבוצי vs דתי-לאומי vs חרדי vs ערבי-ישראלי. Each cluster has different visual codes.
7. **Competitor creatives** — 3 they like (and why), 3 they hate (and why) — gives the visual North Star
8. **Must-include legal** — קרדיט בנק תנאים, ביטוח אחריות, מחיר עם מע"מ, "תקנון באתר", regulated industries (פיננסים, ביטוח, רפואה אסתטית) have on-screen disclaimer rules
9. **Must-include content** — price, phone number, website URL, QR code, kosher symbol, accessibility symbol
10. **Tone** — בטחוני / חמים / שעשעני / יוקרתי / זול-ומשתלם / מקצועי-קר / חוף ים / מסיבה
11. **Budget per asset** — sets the quality bar. Stock-based ₪400 looks different than custom-shoot ₪3500.
12. **Deadline + variants needed** — testing 5 variants of one concept ≠ 5 different concepts

## Format spec library (2026 — verified)

### Meta Feed (Facebook + Instagram)
- **Square 1:1** — `1080×1080` — universal, safe choice
- **Vertical 4:5** — `1080×1350` — **best feed real estate**, biggest mobile footprint (use as default)
- **Link ad 1.91:1** — `1200×628` — for link previews and right-rail
- Safe zone: keep critical elements 60px from each edge
- Text-in-image: Meta no longer caps text %, but >40% text coverage still suppresses reach algorithmically. Aim ≤30%.

### Meta Stories + Reels
- **Vertical 9:16** — `1080×1920`
- **Safe zone top** — 250px (profile pic + name overlay)
- **Safe zone bottom** — 250px (CTA button + reactions bar)
- Hook lives in the **top third** (250-700px from top) — that's where the eye lands
- Critical: design the "thumbnail" frame (frozen first frame) — it's what gets shown in feed before play

### Carousel (Meta + LinkedIn)
- 2–10 cards, `1080×1080` each (or `1080×1350` vertical cards on Meta)
- **Storytelling carousel** — each card progresses (problem → agitate → solution → proof → CTA)
- **Grid mosaic carousel** — cards combine into one wide image when posted as a feed grid (advanced; requires precise edge alignment)

### TikTok Ads
- **Vertical 9:16** — `1080×1920`
- Hook with on-screen text in **first 1.5 seconds** — assume sound off until proven otherwise
- Native UGC style mandatory. Studio-shot ads die here.
- "Spark Ads" repurpose organic posts — design organics that could be promoted

### YouTube Ads
- **Horizontal 16:9** — `1920×1080` for in-stream
- **Vertical 9:16** — `1080×1920` for Shorts
- **Bumper (6s, non-skip)** — needs a single hammer-blow visual + one line of text
- **Skippable (15-30s)** — needs the first 5s to earn the next 25s

### Google Display Network (responsive)
Provide the full set:
- `1200×628` (landscape)
- `1200×1200` (square)
- `1200×300` (leaderboard)
- `600×600` (small square)
- `600×314` (small landscape)
- `300×600` (half-page / skyscraper)
- Plus logo `1200×1200` + landscape logo `1200×300`

### WhatsApp Status Ads (Meta-served)
- **Vertical 9:16** — `1080×1920`, same safe zones as Stories

## Visual hook techniques (the first 0.3s)

The hook is the single frame that stops the scroll. Pick ONE per ad — do not stack.

- **Big bold typographic hook** — first frame is ALL TEXT, no image. Works on Reels/TikTok. e.g. "הפסקתי לעבוד ב-9-5" in Suez One 140pt on a flat color block.
- **Pattern interrupt** — upside-down product, hand entering frame, object mid-air, broken-fourth-wall direct address.
- **Contrast** — bright product on muted neutral background; or muted product on saturated color block.
- **Native-feel** — looks like a friend's iPhone photo: imperfect framing, available light, real apartment in the background.
- **Face shot with eye contact** — direct gaze into camera triggers ~23% higher engagement on Meta. Face fills 40-60% of frame.
- **Motion in first frame** — subtle zoom-in (Ken Burns), particles drifting, smoke, water droplets, hand-held jiggle.
- **Numbers / stats on screen** — "עליתי ב-127% תוך 60 יום". Number in big Latin font, context in Hebrew below.
- **Question on screen** — "חיפשת את זה?" / "למה אף אחד לא סיפר לך?" — questions force the brain to process.

## Hebrew typography for ads

Ad typography ≠ web typography. For ads you want **HIGH IMPACT** that reads at thumbnail size.

**Display fonts** (headlines, hooks — 80-140pt on 1080×1080):
- **Suez One** — chunky, modern, free on Google Fonts. Workhorse for Israeli ad headlines.
- **FbReforma Black / Heavy** — premium foundry font (פונט ברדיפה); ultra-bold news/editorial feel
- **Ploni Heavy** — geometric, clean, contemporary
- **Heebo Black 900** — free, ubiquitous, gets the job done
- **Rubik Mono One** — display monospace for techy/Gen-Z brands
- **Almoni Tzar Black** — narrow display, great for vertical Reels/Stories where horizontal space is precious
- **Frank Ruhl Libre Black** — when you need elegance/luxury (jewelry, wine, hotels)

**Body fonts in ads** (subheads, captions — 32-48pt):
- **Heebo 500-700**, **Assistant 500-600**, **Rubik 500-600**

**Rules:**
- 1 display font + 1 body font. **NEVER 3 fonts.**
- Size hierarchy on 1080×1080: hook 80-140pt / sub-hook 48-64pt / body 32-48pt / CTA 28-36pt / legal 16-20pt
- RTL alignment is the default, but **centered headlines work great in ads** (creative break allowed)
- **Number contrast** — render numbers in a Latin font (Inter, SF Pro Display, Söhne) for tighter kerning. Standard Israeli design trick: "₪199" in Inter feels sharper than in Heebo.
- Track headlines tight (-2% to -4%); body normal (0%); CTA slightly loose (+2%).

## Color systems for ad creative

- **60 / 30 / 10 rule** — 60% neutral background, 30% brand color, 10% accent (typically the CTA)
- **CTA must POP** — measure contrast against background. If brand color = CTA color, the CTA disappears. Pick an accent specifically for buttons.
- **Platform color biases** (real performance data):
  - **TikTok** — high saturation, neon, gradients work. Muted dies.
  - **LinkedIn** — muted, professional, navy/grey/white wins
  - **Instagram** — anything photogenic
  - **Facebook (older audience)** — trust colors win: blue, green, white. Red and black under-perform on conversion ads.
  - **Pinterest** — pastels, beige, "aesthetic" palettes
- **Israeli market visual codes**:
  - Blue + white = "clean / nationalistic / default" — safe but generic
  - Warm earth tones (terracotta, sage, cream, oat) = food, wellness, premium kids' brands
  - Electric / neon (yellow, hot pink, electric blue) = tech, Gen-Z, mobile-first products
  - Black + gold = luxury, jewelry, real estate, automotive
  - Olive + cream + burgundy = boutique food, wine, fine dining

## Composition principles

- **Rule of thirds** — primary subject at intersection, never dead-center (unless intentional symmetry play)
- **Leading lines** — diagonal beats horizontal beats vertical for energy
- **Focal point hierarchy** — eye should know where to land in <1s. One hero element, everything else supports.
- **Negative space** — breathing room around the focal element. Cramped = cheap.
- **Asymmetric > centered** in most cases (centered = forgettable formula)
- **Foreground / midground / background layering** — depth signals quality. Flat = amateur.
- **Z-pattern** (LTR) or **reverse-Z** (RTL Hebrew) for static layouts — eye scans top-right → top-left → bottom-right → bottom-left in Hebrew

## Photography direction

### Native iPhone aesthetic (the default in 2026)
- Available light, no studio strobes
- Shot from chest-height, slight tilt, NOT tripod-perfect
- Shallow but not extreme bokeh (iPhone Portrait mode level, not f/1.4 cinema)
- Imperfect framing: hand partially in frame, cropped-mid-object, slight motion blur OK

### Israeli-feeling locations
- Tel Aviv beach at golden hour (cliché but works for fashion/lifestyle)
- Florentin street walls (graffiti, peeling posters) for urban/young brands
- Jerusalem stone walls + warm light for premium/heritage brands
- Negev desert landscape for adventure/outdoor/automotive
- Israeli cafe interior — wood, plants, אגרטל פרחים, פנקייק על צלחת לבנה
- שוק (Carmel/Mahane Yehuda) energy for food/spice brands
- Apartment kitchen with זוית מצולמת מהמיטה morning light — for wellness/parenting

### People casting
- Cast for the **actual customer**, not a stock model
- Match age, body type, style, ethnicity of the target buyer
- Include real Israeli diversity: אשכנזי, מזרחי, אתיופי, ערבי, רוסי, חרדי, דתי-לאומי when relevant
- **Wardrobe rule**: characters dress one notch ABOVE the target audience's average — aspirational but recognizable

### Wardrobe & props per vertical
- **Cafe / food** — ceramic mug (not paper), MacBook (not Lenovo), linen napkin, חמניות in vase, marble or wood table
- **Fitness** — visible sweat, real gym equipment (not pristine home dumbbells), water bottle, towel, mid-rep capture
- **Legal / professional** — bookshelves, warm desk lamp, fountain pen, leather folder, soft window light from side
- **Beauty / cosmetics** — bathroom counter with real products around, post-shower hair, towel on shoulder
- **Tech / SaaS** — multiple monitors, sticky notes, coffee, Notion/Linear visible on screen, plants

### Stock photos to AVOID (instant amateur signal)
- Laptop + coffee + sunset cliché
- Diverse business team pointing at a screen
- Handshake over a desk
- Doctor pointing at MRI with stethoscope
- Person jumping in the air with arms spread (real estate ads love this)
- Calculator + coins + small house figurine (mortgage ads)
- Anything from Shutterstock front-page

## AI image generation prompts (Nano Banana / Gemini / Midjourney / SDXL)

**Critical limitation:** AI image models butcher Hebrew text. **Always render Hebrew text as a separate layer in Figma/Photoshop on top of the AI image.** Generate the image WITHOUT text, then composite text in post.

### Template 1 — Lifestyle ad shot
```
[Subject doing action] in [Israeli-feeling location], shot on iPhone 15 Pro,
golden hour natural light from [direction], 35mm equivalent lens, shallow depth of field,
candid moment, authentic [age/ethnicity/wardrobe] person, [emotion] expression,
warm color grade, slight film grain, negative space on [left/right/top] for text overlay.
--ar 4:5 --style raw --no extra fingers, plastic skin, oversaturated colors,
symmetrical face, stock photo composition, watermarks
```

### Template 2 — Product hero shot
```
[Product name] on [surface — marble / oak / linen / concrete], studio softbox lighting
from upper-left at 45°, 85mm macro lens, f/4 depth of field, subtle reflection underneath,
[supporting prop 1] and [supporting prop 2] in soft-focus background,
[brand color] accent in foreground, editorial commercial photography,
3:2 framing with negative space top-right for headline overlay.
--ar 1:1 --no harsh shadows, plastic look, oversaturated, generic stock,
floating products, symmetrical layout
```

### Template 3 — Hebrew text-in-image (composited)
1. Generate base image with AI using Template 1 or 2 — **NO TEXT**
2. Open in Figma, place text layer:
   - Hook: Suez One / FbReforma Black, 100-140pt, brand-accent color
   - Sub: Heebo 600, 36-48pt, neutral
   - CTA: Pill button, brand-accent fill, white text Heebo 700 28pt
3. Add subtle drop shadow on text only if background contrast < AA

### Template 4 — Editorial / lifestyle
```
Documentary-style photograph of [scene], shot on Fujifilm X-T5 with 23mm f/1.4,
window light from camera-left, muted color palette of [warm beige / sage / terracotta],
real-life imperfections, candid composition, person not looking at camera,
Israeli apartment interior with [plant / book / שטיח] visible,
shallow depth of field, soft natural shadows.
--ar 9:16 --style raw --no studio lighting, model-look, retouched skin,
artificial perfection, AI-generated symmetry
```

### Template 5 — Studio still-life
```
Overhead flat-lay of [product + 3 supporting props], shot on [linen / marble / wood] surface,
diffused softbox from above, 50mm lens, f/8 deep focus,
geometric composition with [color block / shape] in [position],
brand palette: [hex1] [hex2] [hex3], editorial magazine aesthetic.
--ar 1:1 --no clutter, busy backgrounds, multiple light sources,
amateur shadows, generic prop placement
```

**Universal negative prompts** to append everywhere:
`extra fingers, six fingers, plastic skin, oversaturated, frontal symmetry, generic stock photo, watermark, signature, blurry, low resolution, distorted faces, malformed hands, AI-look, fake bokeh`

## Motion / video brief format

When briefing a video editor (or shooting yourself), provide:

### Storyboard (15-second standard)
- **0.0 – 1.0s — HOOK** — visual + on-screen text. Either pattern interrupt, face + eye contact, or big typographic statement.
- **1.0 – 3.0s — PROBLEM** — agitate the pain. UGC-style talking head OR text overlay OR demonstration of the broken state.
- **3.0 – 6.0s — SOLUTION** — product reveal, transformation, mechanism shown.
- **6.0 – 12.0s — PROOF** — testimonial clip, before/after, stat overlay, screenshot of results.
- **12.0 – 15.0s — CTA** — clear single ask + URL/swipe-up cue + brand mark.

### Specs to deliver
- Aspect ratio (9:16 / 1:1 / 16:9) + duration (6s bumper, 15s, 30s, 60s)
- On-screen text overlays per second — Hebrew copy, font, position, fade-in/out timing
- Music guidance — Israeli libraries: **Artlist Hebrew library**, **Epidemic Sound**, **Musicbed**. Specify mood (upbeat / chill / cinematic / lo-fi) + BPM range.
- **Captions HARDCODED** — burn into video, not platform-generated. 85% of Meta video is watched muted; if it doesn't work silent, it doesn't work.
- **First-frame thumbnail** — the static image shown before autoplay. Treat this as a separate static ad design — it has to stop the scroll on its own.

## Carousel storytelling patterns

- **Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA** — 4-card classic. Card 1 names the pain, card 2 reveals the fix, card 3 shows proof (numbers, testimonial), card 4 is the ask.
- **Before / After** — 2-card or single split-image. Powerful for fitness, design, renovation, אסתטיקה.
- **Educational listicle** — "5 טעויות שעושים בקניית דירה", "7 טיפים לעור זוהר". Card 1 = title, cards 2-6 = each point, card 7 = CTA.
- **Grid mosaic** — designed so cards combine into one big image when posted on the IG feed grid (3-wide). Advanced; requires pixel-precise edge alignment.
- **Catalog** — product 1 / product 2 / product 3 / "shop now". For e-com when you have multiple SKUs that share an audience.

## Production tools workflow

- **Figma** — primary design system, batch export, variant generation, client review
- **Canva** — fast client-editable variations (when client wants to self-serve later)
- **Photoshop** — product photography retouching, complex compositing
- **CapCut** — fast vertical video editing, native captions, transitions
- **Premiere Pro** — long-form video, polished cuts
- **AfterEffects** — motion graphics, kinetic text reveals, brand logo animations
- **Runway Gen-3 / Pika** — AI video clips when live footage unavailable
- **Nano Banana** (Gemini MCP) — AI still-image generation; composite Hebrew text in Figma after
- **Topaz Photo AI** — upscaling, denoising AI-generated assets to print quality

## Self-check before delivery

- [ ] Hook works at 0.3s — cover the bottom 60% with a hand. Does the top 40% still stop the scroll?
- [ ] Logo present but not dominant — max 5–8% of canvas area
- [ ] CTA visible on **mobile thumbnail size** — view at 200px wide; can you still read the CTA?
- [ ] Brand colors used (correct hex), brand fonts used (no system fallbacks)
- [ ] No platform safe-zone violations — pull up the Meta/TikTok mockup, confirm
- [ ] **Mobile-first test** — AirDrop to phone, open in IG/TikTok preview, NOT Figma desktop
- [ ] Text contrast WCAG AA (4.5:1) on all overlays — check with Stark or Figma's contrast plugin
- [ ] Native-feel test for TikTok/Reels — could this be mistaken for organic UGC? Aim YES.
- [ ] Hebrew text proofread — no נ/ז confusion, no missing מקפים, no broken יו"ד
- [ ] Legal disclaimers present in regulated verticals (banking, insurance, aesthetic medicine, real estate)
- [ ] Filenames follow convention; folder zipped per platform

| Carousel set (5–10 cards) | 1,500–3,500 |

Anchor the quality bar to the budget. Premium = custom shoot + bespoke AI prompts + multi-round revisions. Budget = template-based variations off one master concept.

## Anti-patterns (instant amateur signal)

- Cluttered "designed-by-Canva-template" look — 5+ elements fighting for attention
- 3+ fonts in one ad
- Stock photos of people pointing at the camera or laughing at salad
- Gradient blob backgrounds (2021 cliché, dead since 2023)
- Centered logo top + centered CTA bottom — formula = forgettable
- Neon glows on every text element
- Fake "swipe up" indicators or fake play buttons (Meta will reject)
- Overcropped people's heads / cut-off chins
- Text on busy backgrounds without a scrim/blur behind
- 4+ brand colors competing
- Stock-music-loop "corporate uplifting acoustic guitar" on video ads
- Every variant identical except for one word (real variant tests change the IMAGE, not just the copy)
- Logo larger than 10% of canvas
- Three CTAs in one creative — pick ONE ask per ad
