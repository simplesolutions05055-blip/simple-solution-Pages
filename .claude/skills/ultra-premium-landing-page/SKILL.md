# Ultra-Premium Landing Page Designer

The deliverable here is not "a nice landing page." The deliverable is a **landing page that could only belong to this one specific client.** If you could swap the logo and copy and run it for any other business in the same vertical — you failed. Throw it out and start again.

This skill is built around a single non-negotiable: **per-client uniqueness as the core product.** Everything else (conversion, performance, accessibility) is table stakes.

## When to use

**Use `premium-landing-page` instead when:** budget is under ₪7K, deadline is under 5 days, client provides their own brand book and just wants execution, or it's a quick lead-magnet/event LP.

**Use THIS skill when:** project is ₪8K–₪25K, client is a high-value account, Gili has lost a pitch before because the work felt "כמו של כולם", or the LP is the centerpiece of a brand launch.

## 1. Core philosophy — generic is the enemy

Most landing pages in the Israeli market — even "premium" ones — are recycled shadcn templates with a swapped accent color. They share the same fingerprints: purple gradient hero, three-icon feature row, identical pricing cards, founder photo on the right. A client paying ₪15K should never receive that.

**Three rules that govern every decision:**

1. **One client, one visual language.** The design system is grown FROM the client's DNA — not selected from a dropdown. Fonts, motion, color, grid, photography direction — all derived.
2. **Reference brands are seasoning, not entrées.** We blend 2–3 world-class references (Linear, Aesop, Stripe, Nike, etc.) into something new. Never copy one.
3. **If it could be theirs, it's not yours.** Before delivery, mentally swap logos with a competitor. If it still works → it's generic → restart.

This is what separates Simple Solutions from the ₪3K freelancer market. Charge accordingly.

## 2. Client DNA discovery — the interview

Run this BEFORE touching code or design. 15–20 questions, delivered as one batch — don't death-march the client with one-by-one.

**A. Brand essence**
1. תאר את המותג שלך ב-3 שמות תואר (לא "מקצועי" / "אמין" — אלה לא עוזרים).
2. מה ההפך של המותג שלך? (אם המותג הוא "Aesop" — ההפך הוא "AliExpress". זה מספר לי המון.)
3. אם המותג שלך היה רכב — איזה דגם, איזו שנה, איזה צבע?
4. אם המותג שלך היה שיר — איזה ז'אנר, איזה אומן?
5. אם המותג שלך היה עיר — תל אביב, קיוטו, ברלין, מילאנו, ניו יורק, ירושלים?

**B. Competitive landscape**
6. שלח 3 דפי נחיתה של מתחרים שאתה **שונא** — ולמה.
7. שלח 3 דפי נחיתה (בכל תחום) שאתה **אוהב** — ולמה.
8. איזה צבע ה-brand שלך **דוחה** מיידית? (אם הוא דוחה ורוד baby-pink — חשוב לדעת.)
9. אילו פונטים מרגישים "לא אתה"? (Comic Sans זה קל. Poppins גם.)

**C. Audience**
10. גיל, מגדר, רמת הכנסה, רמת השכלה של הלקוח הטיפוסי.
11. מה הם קוראים, מה הם צופים, איפה הם נמצאים אונליין?
12. מה הם פוחדים שאחרים יחשבו עליהם אם יקנו ממך?

**D. Sensory mood**
13. אם הלקוח נכנס למשרד הפיזי שלך — איזה ריח, איזו תאורה, איזה מוזיקה?
14. אם הדף שלך היה מרקם בידיים — מט קטיפתי? זכוכית קרה? נייר ממוחזר? עור איטלקי?
15. אור — קליני וקר, או חם וזהוב, או כחול-כסוף של midnight?

**E. The forbidden zone**
16. מה אסור לי לעשות בעיצוב? (פיוצ'ר-בולטים סטנדרטיים? אווטרים מזויפים? סטוק פוטוז?)
17. מה ההצעה המרכזית — בשורה אחת, בלי buzzwords?
18. מה הפעולה הרצויה — שיחת ייעוץ? קנייה ישירה? הורדת PDF? הרשמה לוובינר?
19. מה התקציב הכולל לפרויקט (כדי לקבע ציפיות לעומק).
20. תאריך השקה רצוי + Hard deadline.

**Output of this step:** a "Client DNA card" — one-page summary in Hebrew that gets approved before any design work begins.

## 3. Design-language assignment — blend, don't copy

Once the DNA card is approved, pick **2–3 reference brands** from `.claude/references/awesome-design-md/design-md/` and define how they blend.

Available references include: `apple`, `linear.app`, `stripe`, `notion`, `airbnb`, `nike`, `ferrari`, `lamborghini`, `bugatti`, `aesop`-equivalents via `tesla`+`apple`, `framer`, `figma`, `vercel`, `cursor`, `claude`, `cohere`, `mintlify`, `superhuman`, `revolut`, `wise`, `mastercard`, `playstation`, `spotify`, `nvidia`, `webflow`, `pinterest`, `meta`, `intercom`, `mongodb`, `supabase`, `replicate`, `runwayml`, `elevenlabs`, `x.ai`, `mistral.ai`, `together.ai`, `kraken`, `binance`, `coinbase`, `wired`, `theverge`, `vodafone`, `starbucks`, `bmw`, `bmw-m`, `renault`, `spacex`, `uber`, `shopify`, `sanity`, `posthog`, `sentry`, `resend`, `clay`, `composio`, `lovable`, `minimax`, `miro`, `slack`, `zapier`, `voltagent`, `clickhouse`, `hashicorp`, `ibm`, `ollama`, `opencode.ai`, `raycast`, `warp`, `expo`, `airtable`, `cal`.

**Rule:** Read each chosen reference's markdown before writing tokens. Extract its rules — type scale, motion easing, grid, color logic, photography direction — then blend.

**Worked example #1 — lawyer for tech founders, Herzliya Pituach:**
- Blend: `stripe` (clarity, density of information done elegantly) + `linear.app` (monochrome restraint, micro-grid backgrounds) + 15% `apple` (oversized weight headlines, hairline dividers).

**Worked example #2 — vegan restaurant in פלורנטין:**
- Blend: `aesop`-via-`apple`-editorial + `airbnb` warmth + 20% `webflow` playfulness.
- Result: `#F4EFE4` cream background, `#2A3B1F` deep moss, `#C75A2C` terracotta accent, display in `Suez One` or `FbReforma Display`, body in `Assistant` 400, hand-drawn SVG vegetable line illustrations, asymmetric stacked layouts, generous editorial whitespace, photography style = warm tungsten close-ups of food on linen.

**Worked example #3 — B2B SaaS by ex-8200 founders:**

- Result: deep `#0B0B0F` near-black, `#E6E6F0` off-white, lime `#B8FF6B` accent, display `Inter Display` ExtraBold tracked tight, body `Inter` 14/22, scroll-jacking demo of product UI, glassmorphism panels at 4% opacity with 1px borders, animated noise grain at 3% over hero.

## 4. Anti-template moves — escape the SaaS look

Concrete techniques. Pick 3–5 per project, never zero:

- **Custom SVG hero illustration** drawn for THIS client (not Storyset, not unDraw, not Lottie marketplace).
- **Unexpected typography pairing** — serif display + mono body, or stencil display + humanist sans body. Avoid Poppins / Inter / Montserrat as the display face (they're SaaS-template tells).
- **Asymmetric grids** — 5/7 split, off-center hero, vertical headline that breaks the column.
- **Full-bleed editorial spreads** — magazine-style sections with image bleed to viewport edge, oversized pull quotes.
- **Color outside the palette of safety** — Yves Klein blue `#002FA7`, deep oxblood `#4A1518`, sage `#9CAE8A`, electric chartreuse `#DFFF00`, hospital green `#A8C5B8`. Anything but generic indigo/violet shadcn defaults.
- **Kinetic typography** — headline that animates word-by-word on scroll, or characters that shuffle on hover.
- **Scroll-triggered storytelling** — sticky section that progresses through 3 states as the user scrolls (use Framer Motion `useScroll` + `useTransform`).
- **Texture overlays** — 3% noise grain, paper texture multiply blend, or subtle film-grain on imagery.
- **Cursor as design element** — custom cursor on hero (a sliding hairline, a magnetic dot, a contextual label).
- **Single hero word** at 18–24vw filling the screen, then aggressive contrast drop into body.
- **Real founder photography** — shot for THIS project, even if iPhone. Never UI Faces, never Pexels.
- **One unexpected interaction** — a draggable element, a tactile slider, a click-to-reveal — that pays off the brand promise.

## 5. Tailored anatomy — five page architectures

Stop using one 14-section template. Match architecture to offer type.

**A. High-ticket service (₪10K+ engagements — lawyer, consultant, surgeon, agency)**
```
1. Nav (logo + single CTA)
2. Hero (manifesto headline, no product shot, single CTA)
3. Credibility wall (client logos OR press logos OR named credentials)
4. The thesis (a 200-word manifesto / philosophy section)
5. Selected work (3–5 case studies with real outcomes, narrative not bullets)
6. Process (3–5 phases, written in first person)
7. The founder (photo, story, why they do this)
8. Testimonials (LONG-form 80-word quotes, not 15-word slogans)
9. Engagement options (2–3 tiers, no "click to chat" — schedule call)
10. FAQ (objections specific to high-ticket buyers)
11. Final CTA + booking widget
```

**B. Impulse-buy product (₪50–₪500, e-commerce, beauty, food)**
```
1. Hero with product photography + price + "Buy now"
2. Three-benefit strip
3. Editorial story (why the product exists)
4. Ingredients / specs / details (visual breakdown)
5. UGC wall (real customer photos)
6. Reviews aggregate (star count + 5 long reviews)
7. Pairings / bundle suggestions
8. Buy block with size/variant picker
9. Shipping & returns reassurance
10. Final hero echo
```

**C. Lead magnet (PDF, checklist, free webinar)**
```
1. Hero with magnet mockup + 1-field email form
2. What's inside (3 bullets MAX)
3. Author credibility (one paragraph)
4. Echo CTA
```
That's it. Four sections. Anything more kills conversion.

**D. Event / launch (one-time)**
```
1. Hero with date, location, hero image, register CTA
2. Countdown + spots remaining
3. The agenda / lineup
4. Speakers / hosts grid
5. Venue + logistics
6. Past attendees / past events social proof
7. Tiered tickets (early bird, regular, VIP)
8. FAQ (parking, kosher, dress code, refund policy)
9. Final register CTA
```

**E. SaaS trial / signup**
```
1. Hero with product UI screenshot + headline + email-only signup
2. The "what it does in 30 seconds" (animated GIF or Loom)
3. Three killer features (with real UI shots, not icons)
4. Integration logos
5. Live testimonial with metric
6. Pricing (3 tiers, annual toggle, free trial CTA)
7. Security & compliance reassurance
8. Comparison vs. status quo
9. Final CTA
```

Mix and match — but justify every section. If a section has no job, kill it.

## 6. Hebrew RTL — the full treatment

This is where 90% of "premium" Israeli LPs visibly fall apart. Don't.

**HTML root:**
```html
<html lang="he" dir="rtl">
```

**Tailwind logical properties** — use these, not directional:
- `ps-*` / `pe-*` instead of `pl-*` / `pr-*`
- `ms-*` / `me-*` instead of `ml-*` / `mr-*`
- `start-*` / `end-*` instead of `left-*` / `right-*`
- `text-start` / `text-end` instead of `text-left` / `text-right`
- `border-s` / `border-e` instead of `border-l` / `border-r`

**Hebrew font stack — pick per mood:**
| Mood | Display | Body |
|---|---|---|
| Tech / startup | `Heebo` 700–900 | `Heebo` 400 |
| Editorial / boutique | `Suez One`, `FbReforma Display` | `Assistant` 400 |
| Bold / direct | `Ploni Bold AAA` | `Ploni Regular AAA` |
| Luxury / serif feel | `FbReforma Narrow`, `Frank Ruhl Libre` | `Assistant` 400 |
| Playful / consumer | `Rubik` | `Rubik` 400 |
| Editorial-magazine | `Karantina` display | `Assistant` 300 |

Load via `next/font/google` or self-host `@fontsource/heebo`. Never use Google Fonts CDN in production (privacy + latency).

**Numeric pockets — keep LTR:**
```html
<span dir="ltr" class="tabular-nums">050-1234567</span>
<span dir="ltr">₪1,290</span>
```

**Line-height adjustments — Hebrew needs more breathing room than English:**
- Display: `leading-tight` → `leading-[1.15]` (Hebrew descenders demand it)
- Body: `leading-relaxed` → `leading-[1.7]`
- Tight UI labels: `leading-[1.3]` minimum

**Punctuation:**
- Hebrew uses `״` (gershayim) and `׳` (geresh) — not `"` `'`. Configure Markdown/CMS to preserve.
- Avoid hyphenation in Hebrew — disable `hyphens: auto` on `<html lang="he">`.

**Form validation messages — Hebrew, friendly, never "Error":**
- `שדה חובה` (not `שדה לא תקין`)
- `נראה שיש טעות במייל — בדקי שוב?`
- `הטלפון חייב להתחיל ב-05`

## 8. Code stack rules

**Mandatory stack:**
- Next.js 15 App Router + TypeScript (strict mode)
- Tailwind CSS 4 (CSS-first, no `tailwind.config.js` if avoidable — use `@theme` block)
- shadcn/ui (only the primitives — Button, Input, Dialog, Accordion. Restyle, never ship default.)
- Framer Motion 11+ for entrance + scroll choreography
- `next/font` for Hebrew fonts (self-hosted)
- `next/image` for ALL imagery, with explicit `sizes`

**File structure:**
```
app/
  (lp)/
    [client-slug]/
      page.tsx              ← server component, SEO meta
      layout.tsx            ← per-LP fonts + dir="rtl"
      _components/
        Hero.tsx
        Proof.tsx
        Thesis.tsx
        ...
      _content/
        copy.ts             ← all Hebrew copy, typed
        tokens.css          ← per-client CSS variables
      _assets/
        hero.webp
        og.png
        favicon.svg
api/
  lead/route.ts             ← POST handler → Supabase + Resend
  cardcom/notify/route.ts   ← Cardcom IPN callback
DESIGN-LANGUAGE.md
STRUCTURE.md
```

**Performance targets (hard requirements before delivery):**
- LCP ≤ 1.8s on Slow 4G
- CLS ≤ 0.05
- INP ≤ 200ms
- TBT ≤ 200ms
- Total transfer ≤ 1.2MB (hero image ≤ 280KB after AVIF/WebP)
- Lighthouse: Performance ≥95, Accessibility ≥95, Best Practices 100, SEO 100

**Non-negotiables:**
- `next/image` everywhere with explicit `priority` on hero, `loading="lazy"` elsewhere
- Preload Hebrew display font with `display: swap`
- Critical CSS inlined (Next.js does this; verify)
- No 3rd-party scripts in head — defer GA/Meta to `next/script` strategy="afterInteractive"
- Self-host every font; never Google Fonts CDN

## 9. Forms & CRM integration

**Default architecture (production):**
- Form → Next.js Server Action → Supabase `leads` table + Resend transactional email to Gili + customer auto-reply
- UTM capture on first page view → stored in cookie → submitted with form
- Meta Pixel `Lead` event + GA4 `generate_lead` event fired on success (client-side after server confirmation)
- Honeypot field (`<input name="website" tabindex="-1" autocomplete="off" hidden>`) + 3-second min-time-to-submit to block bots

**Hebrew validation copy (use these — don't invent):**
- שם: `איך לקרוא לך?` (placeholder), `נשמח לדעת איך לפנות אלייך` (error)
- טלפון: `נייד לחזרה`, `הטלפון צריך להתחיל ב-05 ולהיות 10 ספרות`
- אימייל: `אימייל לאישור`, `נראה שיש שם טעות — בדקי?`

**For paid LPs (checkout flow) — Cardcom is the default (NEVER Stripe in Israel):**
- Cardcom LowProfile redirect or iframe
- Pass `ReturnValue` = lead ID from Supabase
- Cardcom IPN webhook → `/api/cardcom/notify/route.ts` → update lead status to paid → fire Meta `Purchase` event server-side via Conversions API
- Backup payment: Grow / משולם as secondary option for clients who prefer

**Lead destination dashboard:**
- Quick LP (1-week turnaround): Formspree → Gili's email
- Production LP: Supabase `leads` table + Gili gets Slack/Telegram notification via Resend → Make.com webhook

## 10. Self-uniqueness audit — run before delivery

Print this checklist. Walk through every item. If any FAIL, restart that section.

**The swap test:**
- [ ] Mentally replace the logo with a direct competitor's logo. Does the page still feel right? → If YES, FAIL. The page is generic.
- [ ] Mentally replace ALL copy with lorem ipsum. Does the visual language still tell you what industry this is? → If NO, the design isn't telling the story. FAIL.

**The fingerprint test:**
- [ ] Display font is NOT Poppins, Inter, Montserrat, or Roboto (default SaaS tells).
- [ ] Brand color is NOT default Tailwind `indigo-600`, `violet-600`, or shadcn `zinc`.
- [ ] No three-icon-feature-row that looks like every Webflow template.
- [ ] No fake testimonial avatars from UI Faces / RandomUser. Real photos or no photos.
- [ ] No "Lorem ipsum dolor" stock-feel placeholder photography (people on laptops, handshakes, abstract gradient blobs).

**The DNA test:**
- [ ] Each design decision traces back to ONE answer in the Client DNA card. (Can you justify the color? The font? The motion? If "I just liked it" — restart.)
- [ ] The 2–3 reference brands chosen are visible in the work — but NONE of them is dominant. It's a blend, not a clone.

**The unmistakable detail test:**
- [ ] There is at least ONE element on this page that no other landing page in Israel currently has. Name it out loud. If you can't name it — add one.

**Performance gate:**
- [ ] Lighthouse run on mobile throttled. All four scores ≥95 (Performance, Accessibility, Best Practices, SEO).
- [ ] Tested on a real Android mid-tier phone (not just Chrome devtools).
- [ ] Tested on Safari iOS — RTL + custom fonts especially.

Only after every box is ticked → deliver.

## 13. Anti-patterns — the generic-LP tells

Catch these on yourself before the client catches them on you:

1. **Stock photos of people on laptops** looking at the camera — the universal "we have no real photography" signal.
2. **Gradient blob shapes** in the hero — every SaaS template since 2020.
3. **Default shadcn purple-violet buttons** — `bg-violet-600 hover:bg-violet-700` is a fingerprint.
4. **Three-icon feature row** with Heroicons outline icons + 5-word headline + 1-sentence body.
5. **Fake testimonial avatars** from UI Faces / RandomUser / Generated Photos. Real or nothing.
6. **Pricing cards with the middle one elevated** + a "Most Popular" badge — used in 100% of SaaS templates.
7. **Animated number counters** ("10,000+ clients") that count up on scroll — cliché since 2017.
8. **"Trusted by" logo bar** with greyscale logos of companies the client has never worked with.
9. **Hero subheadline that starts with "We help X do Y"** — the SaaS template formula. Write a real headline.
10. **Founder photo on the right** with arms crossed in a blazer.
11. **Carousels for testimonials** — nobody clicks them. Show 2–3 in a static grid.
12. **Floating WhatsApp button as the only mobile CTA** — lazy. Design a real mobile CTA strategy.
13. **Lorem-ipsum-looking sans-serifs** (Poppins / Inter / Montserrat) as the display face.
14. **Identical bento grids** with rounded-2xl cards — every 2024 landing page.
15. **Generic 404 / thank-you pages** — these are part of the deliverable. Design them.
16. **"Get started" CTA copy** with no destination — what does "get started" mean? Be specific: "תיאום שיחת ייעוץ של 20 דקות".
17. **Hyphenated Hebrew** — `hyphens: auto` left on by mistake. Reads broken.
18. **Underline-only links** in body copy — looks like an unstyled `<a>`. Restyle every link.
19. **Default rounded-full avatars** for team — try squared, hexagonal, or no frame at all.
20. **Hero headline >12 words** — if you can't say it in 8, the offer isn't clear yet.
